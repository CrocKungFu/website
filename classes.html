Find Your Practice

Three training pathways designed to support health, personal development and traditional martial arts training.

Chi Kung • Walking Meditation • Shuang Yang

Yin Practice

A calm and meditative practice focused on breath, posture and mindful movement. Through Chi Kung, Walking Meditation and Shuang Yang, students develop balance, coordination and internal awareness while supporting their overall health and wellbeing.

Kung Fu • Conditioning • Partner Training

Yang Practice

A dynamic class combining traditional Kung Fu, conditioning, stretching and partner practice. Training develops strength, coordination, confidence and practical skill while preserving the depth and traditions of the Nam Yang system.

Movement • Confidence • Respect

Children’s Classes

Children’s classes introduce movement, coordination and traditional Kung Fu through games, exercises and structured activities. Training encourages confidence, respect and healthy development in a supportive environment. Suitable for children aged 6–12.

Classes For Everyone

Whether you’re completely new to Kung Fu and Chi Kung or have years of experience, you’re welcome to join us.

Training is adapted to the individual and focuses on steady progress rather than competition or performance.

Beginners Welcome

No previous experience is required. Many students begin their journey with no martial arts or Chi Kung background at all.

Different Fitness Levels

Training can be adapted to different ages, abilities and personal goals. The emphasis is on progress, not perfection.

Visitors Welcome

Whether you’re visiting Corfu for a week or staying for several months, you’re welcome to join a class and experience the training for yourself.

Train At Your Own Pace

Everyone develops differently. The goal is not to keep up with others but to build a sustainable practice that supports long-term growth.

Why People Keep Coming Back

Many people begin training for health, fitness or curiosity. Over time, they discover that the real value lies in something deeper: showing up, week after week, and allowing the practice to shape them.

After all, if you don’t keep coming back, it’s not really Kung Fu.

Health & Wellbeing

Regular training helps improve mobility, posture, coordination and overall wellbeing. Small improvements accumulate over time, creating lasting benefits both inside and outside of class.

Personal Development

Training develops patience, confidence, discipline and self-awareness through steady and consistent practice. Progress is rarely dramatic, but those who continue to show up often discover how much they have changed.

Community

Classes bring together people from different backgrounds who share a common interest in learning, growing and supporting one another. Many students arrive for the training and stay for the people.
