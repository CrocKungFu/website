A Typical Training Day

Each day follows a natural rhythm that balances training, reflection, recovery and community, creating an environment that supports both personal development and meaningful learning.

Morning Practice

Begin the day with Chi Kung, Walking Meditation and Shuang Yang, developing rooting, grounding, balance and internal awareness through calm and focused practice.

Tea & Discussion

Training pauses for tea, conversation and exploration of the principles and philosophy that underpin the Nam Yang tradition.

Applications & Integration

The morning continues with Shuang Yang applications, helping students understand how movement, structure and partner work come together in practice before concluding with stretching and recovery.

Afternoon Training

The afternoon session develops strength, structure and practical skill through Vein & Tendon Chi Kung, Sum Chien, partner training and guided practice. A tea break offers time to rest and reflect before the session concludes with stretching and recovery work.

Shared Evening Meal

The day concludes with a shared dinner at Thomas' home, where participants enjoy homemade Greek food, conversation and time together as the experiences of the day settle in.

Beyond the Training

Training is only part of the experience. Each retreat includes time to rest, explore and enjoy the natural beauty of Corfu. Whether we're discovering hidden coves by boat, hiking coastal trails, swimming in crystal-clear waters or sharing a sunset meal overlooking the sea, these moments often become some of the most memorable parts of the week.

A Family Tradition

One of the highlights of the retreat. We spend several hours exploring the coastline by boat, visiting sea caves, swimming spots and hidden beaches alongside my family and local friends.

Time to Slow Down

Quiet places, clear water and moments of simple exploration away from the crowds.

Discover Corfu

Optional hikes lead to some of the island's most spectacular viewpoints and unforgettable swims.

Every retreat concludes the day with good company, local food and a sunset worth remembering.

Accommodation & Practical Information

Everything is designed to keep the retreat experience simple and stress-free, allowing you to focus on your training, recovery and time in Corfu.

Accommodation

Private or shared studio accommodation is available at Margarita Pension, located just a few minutes' walk from both the beach and the training venue. Simple, comfortable and conveniently located, it provides an ideal base for the retreat week.

Food & Daily Life

Tea breaks and shared evening meals during training days are included. Dinner is enjoyed together at Thomas' home, featuring homemade Greek food and time to connect with fellow participants. For breakfast and lunch, Arillas offers a wide variety of cafés, bakeries and restaurants within easy walking distance, catering to most dietary preferences.

Getting Here

The nearest airport is Corfu International Airport (CFU). Airport transfers can be arranged upon request. Arillas is approximately one hour from the airport by car.

No previous martial arts experience is required. Training is adapted to different ages, abilities and levels of experience.
